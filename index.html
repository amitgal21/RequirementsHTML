Introduction

Today there are 35 million diagnosed cancer patients in the world. As part of the treatment process for the disease, it is required to stay in a quarantine period that ranges from 3 days to 4 months in which the patient has no contact with family members, friends, or the outside environment. Cancer patients are not allowed to bring in any electrical device such as a telephone or television. may put them at risk because their immune system is unstable.
Our Application Goals Are:

  • To eliminate the feeling of loneliness and boredom.
  • To create a permanent relationship with the family during the quarantine.
  • To increase in the quality of treatment and the patient's stay in the hospitals.

In our project we are producing a system called Isolation Busters whose goal is to relieve the loneliness and boredom of cancer patients in addition to producing a system that will allow the user to be in constant contact with his family members through video calls and regular calls, also the system will support games, music, video reading articles and a variety of other things Wide range of options.

Result

During the project, we saw that the most difficult thing for the patients to deal with is alleviating the feeling of boredom and the severe feeling of loneliness. Another but surprising finding is that cancer patients are not interested in communicating with other cancer patients and this was a contradiction to one of the demands we made during the construction of the project, this made us give up this option because there is no real need for it. You can also see in the interviews and feedback that our application is a basic need for the patients and the hospitals in the Cape, we can make the patients happy and also raise the level of quality of the hospital that will be able to provide a mental response to its patients.

Challenges

One of the most difficult challenges that arose in the construction of the project is which architecture to use because today there is a wide variety of technologies that do not universally support a certain type of software, so after much thought we decided that we were interested in using a client-server architecture that sat on a tablet. We also added the option to download a music/game Personally, that's why we also decided that the server would sit on the cloud.
